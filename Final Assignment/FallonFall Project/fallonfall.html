If you dig Bedhead, the Fall, Pavement, Sonic Youth, Built to Spill, Polvo, Rolling Rock.

From the ashes of the Revolutionary Hydra and SMRK comes a righteous band of old dudes who believe in the inalienable right to cheap yet refreshing beer, deodorant, and who believe that sensitive men who write songs can be in a band that doesn't suck. This is that band. Fall on Fall will reignite your love for 90's era indie rock. This is a promise. Anyone who does not like the band will be issued one beer. (this beer may be warm or of a "cheap" variety, as it will be at the discretion of the label owner. buyer beware.

About these Gentlemen

A word or two from indie-rock impresario Anton Filigree... One of the better-known groups I managed, The Revolutionary Hydra, contained a certain Barton Sharp. Several fans of the Hydra always seemed frustrated at the Harrison-esque songwriting quota from Bart. Good news for them, then, is Bartonʼs new band, Fall on Fall. Those familiar with Barton Sharpʼs background have seen a progression from the charming “sure thatʼll work” Appleseed Apart years growing up in Battleground WA to the inchoate “sure thatʼll work” basement brilliance of his Revolutionary Hydra stint in Bellingham WA. Several years hence, following a period of Seattle urbanite rumination and songwriting hibernation, itʼs clear that Bart formed, during this indie interregnum, a vision To realize this project Bart assembled a whoʼs who of Seattle talent. Behind the kit and caboodle, Robbie Skrocki brings a veteranʼs power and precision with his inimitablePabst Blue Collar directness. Manning the lanky bass is Seattle subterranean mise-en-scènester Brian Willett, contributing recording duties and a multiversalist sensibility, with his pop, punk and bossa nova backgrounds.
